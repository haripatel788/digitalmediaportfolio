Car Commercial

Skills: I learned how to record, cut, and layer audio tracks efficiently in Adobe Audition. I practiced using fade-ins/outs, automation, and multitrack editing.

Editing decisions: I trimmed down unnecessary pauses, adjusted timing for dramatic effect, and added a background engine rev to emphasize the car’s power.

Audio elements used: I used music beds, sound effects (like a car starting), voiceovers, and reverb to enhance the delivery.

Personal opinion: I was really proud of the final result. It sounded clean and professional, and I liked how the music and voiceover worked together.

Suggestions: I would improve the pacing in some sections and try a different voice modulation to make it more dynamic.

Monologuing

Skills: This helped me improve my voice acting, pacing, and emotional expression. I also practiced using EQ to make my voice clearer.

Editing decisions: I added background ambiance to match the tone and adjusted pitch slightly for clarity. I also normalized the volume for consistency.

Audio elements used: I only used one voice track with subtle reverb and ambient background sound to make it more immersive.

Personal opinion: I liked the storytelling aspect of this piece. It allowed me to experiment with tone and delivery in a meaningful way.

Suggestions: Next time, I’d re-record some lines to better convey emotion and reduce minor background noise.

Radio Ad

Skills: I developed skills in sound layering, syncing dialogue with music, and mastering audio levels.

Editing decisions: I chose an upbeat music track, synced it with the voiceover, and added a stinger at the end to emphasize the brand.

Audio elements used: Voiceover, background music, stingers, and a few sound effects for realism like a cash register sound.

Personal opinion: This was one of my favorite projects. It felt polished and sounded like a real radio ad you'd hear on the air.

Suggestions: I’d refine the transitions a bit more and try to add call-to-action phrases to make it more engaging.
